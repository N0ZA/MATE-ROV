Rules, platform data & method

Surface platform threat — based on the track's closest approach: under 5 nm = red, 5–10 nm = yellow, over 10 nm = green. Override: if keel depth is 110% or more of the water depth at a platform, the berg grounds first → green regardless of distance.

Subsea asset threat — only if the track passes within 25 nm of the platform (otherwise green, “does not intersect”). Within range, by keel-to-depth ratio: ≥110% green (grounds), 90–110% red, 70–90% yellow, under 70% green (keel too shallow).

Distance uses 1′ latitude = 1 nm, with east–west distance scaled by cos(latitude) (≈0.68 here). The printed competition map has square cells, so a degree of longitude looks the same width as a degree of latitude even though it is only ~41 nm versus 60 nm — measuring a diagonal track with a latitude ruler on paper over-counts the east–west part and can turn a yellow into a red. This console applies the correct longitude scale. Platform values are the updated landscape sheet’s decimals, which reproduce all six official example answer keys.
